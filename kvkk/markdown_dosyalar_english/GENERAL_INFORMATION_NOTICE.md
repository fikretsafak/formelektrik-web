## 1. PURPOSE

This Information Notice aims to explain the principles regarding the lawful processing of personal data, in accordance with **Personal Data Protection Law No. 6698** (the **“PDPL”**) and related legislation, for all real persons who come into contact with **Form Elektrik İnşaat Mühendislik Anonim Şirketi** (the **“Company”**), including but not limited to customers, visitors, suppliers, subcontractors, employees / shareholders of suppliers or subcontractors, prospective buyers of products and services, and persons using the Company’s website and network connections.

Because you may be a real person customer, visitor, supplier, subcontractor, employee of a supplier / subcontractor, shareholder, prospective product or service buyer, or another real person in contact with the Company, the personal data that you provide to the Company, or that are otherwise obtained by the Company, may be recorded, stored, preserved, reorganized, shared with institutions legally authorized to request such data, transferred to domestic third parties under the conditions prescribed by the PDPL, assigned, classified and otherwise processed by the Company in its capacity as **Data Controller**, provided that such processing is:

- related to, limited to and proportionate with the purpose requiring the processing,
- carried out while maintaining the accuracy and up-to-date nature of the personal data as notified to the Company,
- aligned with the principles laid down in the PDPL.

Detailed information regarding the processing of your personal data is available in the Company policy published at `www.formelektrik.com.tr`.

## 3. GENERAL CONDITIONS FOR PROCESSING PERSONAL DATA

Personal data may be processed only in the cases prescribed by law or with the explicit consent of the person concerned. Under Article 5 of the PDPL, personal data may not be processed without explicit consent unless one of the legal grounds listed in the law exists.

### 3.1. Where Explicitly Provided for by Law
Personal data may be processed without consent where the law clearly permits such processing.

### 3.2. Protection of Life or Physical Integrity
Where it is necessary to protect the life or physical integrity of a person who is unable to give consent due to actual impossibility or whose consent is not legally valid, personal data may be processed without explicit consent.

### 3.3. Necessity for the Establishment or Performance of a Contract
Where necessary for the establishment or performance of a contract, personal data belonging to the parties to the contract may be processed without explicit consent.

### 3.4. Necessity for Compliance with a Legal Obligation of the Data Controller
The Company may process personal data without explicit consent where this is necessary for the fulfilment of its legal obligations.

### 3.5. Data Made Public by the Data Subject
If a personal data item has been made public by the data subject, the Company may process that data without explicit consent to the extent required for its activities and purposes.

### 3.6. Necessity for the Establishment, Exercise or Protection of a Right
Personal data may be processed where processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise or protection of a legal right.

### 3.7. Necessity for the Legitimate Interests of the Data Controller
Personal data may be processed where processing is necessary for the legitimate interests of the Company, provided that the fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject are not harmed.

## 4. PROCESSING OF SPECIAL CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL DATA

Special categories of personal data may be processed only under the conditions prescribed in the PDPL and by taking adequate measures determined by the Personal Data Protection Board. Where required by law, the Company will obtain explicit consent or rely on another lawful basis specifically provided for in the legislation.

## 5. METHODS OF COLLECTING AND PROCESSING PERSONAL DATA

Your personal data may be collected and processed verbally, in writing or electronically, through automatic or non-automatic means that form part of a data recording system, including but not limited to:

- website, e-mail, forms and online systems,
- security cameras and physical access points,
- contracts, offer / order documents and invoices,
- customer and supplier records,
- call center, applications, requests and complaints,
- visits, meetings, business relationship processes, and similar channels.

## 6. LEGAL REASONS FOR THE PROCESSING OF PERSONAL DATA

Your personal data may be processed based on one or more of the following legal reasons:

- where explicitly provided for by law,
- where necessary for the establishment or performance of a contract,
- where necessary for the Company to comply with legal obligations,
- where the data have been made public by the data subject,
- where necessary for the establishment, exercise or protection of a right,
- where necessary for the legitimate interests of the Company, provided that fundamental rights and freedoms are not harmed,
- based on explicit consent where required.

## 8. SHARING PERSONAL DATA WITH THIRD PARTIES

Your personal data may be shared, in accordance with the PDPL and relevant legislation, with legally authorized public institutions and organizations, courts and administrative authorities, business partners, suppliers, subcontractors, service providers, auditors, lawyers, consultants, banks, cargo / logistics providers, IT infrastructure providers and other authorized recipients to the extent necessary for the purposes described in this notice.

## 9. RETENTION OF PERSONAL DATA

The Company retains personal data for the period stipulated in the relevant legislation or, if no specific period is provided, for the period required for the purpose of processing.

## 10. DELETION, DESTRUCTION AND ANONYMIZATION OF PERSONAL DATA

Where the reasons requiring processing cease to exist, personal data are deleted, destroyed or anonymized ex officio or upon request, in accordance with the legislation and the Company’s retention and destruction policy.

## 11. RIGHTS OF THE DATA SUBJECT AND EXERCISE OF SUCH RIGHTS

### 11.1. Rights of the Data Subject
Within the scope of Article 11 of the PDPL, the data subject has the right to:

- learn whether personal data are being processed,
- request information if personal data have been processed,
- learn the purpose of processing and whether the data are used in accordance with that purpose,
- know the third parties to whom personal data are transferred domestically or abroad,
- request correction of incomplete or inaccurate personal data,
- request deletion / destruction / anonymization where the conditions are met,
- request notification of such corrections or deletions to third parties to whom the data have been transferred,
- object to the emergence of a result against the person through analysis by automated systems,
- request compensation if damage is suffered due to unlawful processing.
