## 1. PURPOSE

According to Article 20 of the Constitution of the Republic of Türkiye, everyone has the right to demand respect for private and family life and the protection of personal data relating to them. This right includes being informed about personal data concerning the individual, accessing such data, requesting their correction or deletion, and learning whether they are used in accordance with their purposes.

This Policy has been prepared to ensure that all processes regarding the processing, storage and transfer of personal data obtained by **Form Elektrik İnşaat Mühendislik Anonim Şirketi** (the **“Company”**) from customers, visitors, suppliers, subcontractors, employees of suppliers, potential product and service buyers, shareholders, website users and other persons contacted during business activities are carried out in compliance with **Personal Data Protection Law No. 6698** (the **“PDPL”**) and related legislation.

## 3. GENERAL PRINCIPLES TO BE OBSERVED IN THE PROCESSING OF PERSONAL DATA

The Company processes personal data in compliance with the Constitution of the Republic of Türkiye, the PDPL and all related secondary legislation. The Company acts in accordance with the following principles:

### 3.1. Compliance with Law and Principles of Good Faith
The Company processes personal data lawfully and in good faith.

### 3.2. Accuracy and Up-to-Date Status
The Company takes maximum care to ensure that personal data are accurate and, where necessary, kept up to date.

### 3.3. Processing for Specific, Explicit and Legitimate Purposes
Personal data are processed only for specific, explicit and legitimate purposes related to the Company’s activities.

### 3.4. Being Relevant, Limited and Proportionate
Only personal data necessary for the relevant purpose are processed.

### 3.5. Retention for the Period Required by Legislation or Purpose
Personal data are retained for the period required by applicable legislation or, if no period is set, for the period necessary for the purpose of processing. After the retention period, data are destroyed by deletion, destruction or anonymization.

## 4. GENERAL CONDITIONS FOR PROCESSING PERSONAL DATA

Personal data may be processed in the cases prescribed by law or with explicit consent. The Company may process personal data without explicit consent where one of the legal grounds listed below exists.

### 4.1. Where Explicitly Provided for by Law
### 4.2. Where Necessary to Protect Life or Physical Integrity
### 4.3. Where Necessary for the Establishment or Performance of a Contract
### 4.4. Where Necessary for Compliance with a Legal Obligation of the Data Controller
### 4.5. Where the Data Have Been Made Public by the Data Subject
### 4.6. Where Necessary for the Establishment, Exercise or Protection of a Right
### 4.7. Where Necessary for the Legitimate Interests of the Data Controller

In all such cases, processing is carried out within the limits and conditions prescribed by the PDPL.

## 5. CONDITIONS FOR PROCESSING SPECIAL CATEGORIES OF PERSONAL DATA

Special categories of personal data are processed only in cases permitted under the PDPL, by taking adequate measures determined by the Personal Data Protection Board. Health and sexual life data, as well as other special categories, are processed only where explicit consent exists or where the law specifically permits such processing.

## 6. METHODS AND LEGAL BASES FOR COLLECTING AND PROCESSING PERSONAL DATA

Personal data may be collected verbally, in writing or electronically, through automatic or non-automatic means forming part of a data recording system, including but not limited to application forms, contracts, cameras, information systems, website forms, e-mail, call centers, customer / supplier processes, visitors and legal documents.

The legal bases include the grounds listed in Articles 5 and 6 of the PDPL, especially legal obligation, contractual necessity, legitimate interest, establishment / protection of rights and explicit consent where required.

The Company may process personal data relating to the following groups: customers / potential customers, visitors, suppliers, supplier employees / representatives, subcontractors, shareholders / partners, employees, job applicants, website users, logistics vehicle users, employee relatives and other third parties.

- recruitment and placement,
- management of employment records,
- payroll and personnel affairs,
- occupational health and safety,
- training and qualification tracking,
- performance, assignment and internal organization processes.

### 9.2. Planning and Execution of the Security of the Company and / or Third Parties in Business Relations with the Company
- physical security,
- visitor management,
- information security,
- operational risk management,
- emergency and business continuity processes.

### 9.3. Planning and Execution of Product and / or Service Processes Provided by or on Behalf of the Company
- order, sales and after-sales processes,
- customer services,
- technical support,
- supplier and procurement processes,
- logistics and delivery operations.

### 9.4. Planning and Execution of Commercial and / or Operational Activities Conducted by the Company
- finance and accounting,
- reporting,
- internal audit and control,
- legal affairs,
- communication, request and complaint handling,
- regulatory compliance and documentation.

### 9.5. Planning, Auditing and Execution of Commercial Life and Business Development Strategies
- business development,
- strategic planning,
- corporate governance,
- commercial partnerships,
- marketing and communication activities in compliance with the law.

## 10. TRANSFER OF PERSONAL DATA

### 10.1. Transfer of Personal Data
Personal data may be transferred domestically to authorized public institutions and organizations, courts, administrative authorities, suppliers, service providers, business partners, consultants, auditors, lawyers, financial institutions and other recipients where required by the purpose of processing and the relevant legislation.

### 10.2. Transfer of Special Categories of Personal Data
Special categories of personal data may be transferred only under the strict conditions prescribed by the PDPL and by taking the adequate safeguards required by the legislation.

## 11. RETENTION OF PERSONAL DATA

The Company retains personal data for the period required by the relevant legislation or for the period necessary for the purpose of processing.

## 12. DELETION, DESTRUCTION AND ANONYMIZATION OF PERSONAL DATA

Where the reasons requiring processing cease to exist, personal data are deleted, destroyed or anonymized in accordance with the law and the Company’s retention and destruction policy.

## 13. RECIPIENT GROUPS TO WHICH PERSONAL DATA MAY BE TRANSFERRED AND PURPOSES OF TRANSFER

Recipient groups may include public authorities, judicial authorities, auditors, lawyers, banks, suppliers, subcontractors, business partners, service providers, IT companies, cargo / logistics companies, consultants and other authorized recipients, for purposes such as performing contractual and legal obligations, providing products and services, ensuring security, conducting finance and accounting processes, and carrying out operational and administrative activities.

## 14. INFORMING DATA SUBJECTS

The Company informs data subjects in accordance with Article 10 of the PDPL regarding the identity of the data controller, the purposes of processing, transfer recipients, legal reasons and collection methods, and the rights of the data subject.

## 15. MEASURES TO BE TAKEN FOR THE PROTECTION OF PERSONAL DATA

### 15.1. Administrative Measures
The Company takes administrative measures such as policies and procedures, role and authorization controls, confidentiality commitments, training and awareness studies, internal audits, disciplinary processes, supplier controls and breach management processes.

### 15.2. Technical Measures
The Company takes technical measures such as access controls, authorization matrices, network and application security, logging, encryption, backup, anti-virus systems, firewall configurations, periodic vulnerability and penetration tests, secure storage environments and data loss prevention controls.

### 15.3. Supervision of the Measures Taken
The Company monitors and audits the effectiveness of the measures taken for the protection of personal data and updates them where necessary.

## 16. RIGHTS OF THE DATA SUBJECT AND APPLICATION METHOD

### 16.1. Rights of Data Subjects
Under Article 11 of the PDPL, data subjects have the right to learn whether their personal data are processed, request information, learn processing purposes, learn transfer recipients, request correction, deletion or anonymization, object to automated processing results, and request compensation for damages caused by unlawful processing.
