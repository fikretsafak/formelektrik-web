## 1. DATA CONTROLLER

Your personal data may be processed by **Form Elektrik İnşaat Mühendislik Anonim Şirketi** (the **“Company”**) in its capacity as the data controller, within the scope described below and in accordance with **Personal Data Protection Law No. 6698** (the **“Law”**).

Detailed information regarding the processing of your personal data by our Company can be obtained from our Human Resources Department and from the Company’s internal policy on the protection and processing of personal data.

## 2. PERSONAL DATA PROCESSED IN RELATION TO JOB APPLICANTS

Within the scope of this information notice, the following personal data belonging to job applicants may be processed:

| Data Category | Scope / Examples |
|---|---|
| Identity Data | Full name, Turkish ID number, place of birth, date of birth, marital status, gender, signature/initial information, photograph |
| Contact Data | Telephone number, full address, e-mail address, including corporate e-mail address and internal extension information where applicable |
| Personnel / HR Data | CV / résumé documents, military service status, driver’s license |
| Special Categories of Personal Data | Health data; data relating to criminal convictions and security measures |
| Employment Data | Previous employer information, previous titles and departments, employment start and end dates, travel restriction status, position applied for, information on whether the applicant has any service obligation toward any institution or organization |
| Financial Data | Requested monthly net salary |
| Physical Premises Security Data | Records and documents created during entry to and presence within physical premises |
| Education Data | Educational background, certificates and diplomas, foreign language information, professional qualification information, occupational health and safety training information |
| Family / Relative Information | Information regarding family members and close relatives |
| Other Data | Reference information |

## 3. LEGAL GROUNDS FOR PROCESSING PERSONAL DATA

Although explicit consent is one legal basis for processing personal data, personal data may also be processed without explicit consent where one of the legal grounds set out in the Law exists.

### 3.1. Where Explicitly Provided for by Law
Personal data may be processed where processing is clearly provided for in the Law or in other applicable legislation.

### 3.2. Protection of Life or Physical Integrity
Where it is necessary to protect the life or physical integrity of a person who is unable to give consent due to actual impossibility or whose consent is not legally valid, personal data may be processed without explicit consent.

### 3.3. Necessity for the Establishment or Performance of a Contract
Personal data belonging to the parties to a contract may be processed where such processing is necessary for the establishment or performance of the contract.

### 3.4. Necessity for Compliance with a Legal Obligation
The Company may process personal data where this is necessary for the Company to fulfil its legal obligations.

### 3.5. Personal Data Made Public by the Data Subject
If the data subject has made personal data public, such data may be processed to the extent necessary and in line with the purpose of disclosure.

### 3.6. Necessity for the Establishment, Exercise or Protection of a Right
Personal data may be processed where processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise or protection of a legal right.

### 3.7. Necessity for the Legitimate Interests of the Company
Personal data may be processed where processing is necessary for the legitimate interests of the Company, provided that the fundamental rights and freedoms of the data subject are not harmed.

## 4. PURPOSES OF PROCESSING YOUR PERSONAL DATA

Your personal data may be processed for the following purposes, within the personal data processing conditions set out in Articles 5 and 6 of the Law:

- planning and carrying out recruitment, evaluation and interview processes,
- assessing the applicant’s suitability for the relevant position,
- conducting communication regarding the application process,
- carrying out reference and background checks where relevant,
- determining salary expectations and employment conditions,
- ensuring workplace and physical premises security,
- planning human resources processes,
- fulfilling legal obligations arising from labor, occupational health and safety and other legislation,
- handling potential disputes and exercising legal rights,
- maintaining records, archives and reporting processes,
- evaluating the applicant for future positions, where appropriate.

## 5. PARTIES TO WHOM YOUR PERSONAL DATA MAY BE TRANSFERRED AND PURPOSES OF TRANSFER

Your personal data may be transferred, in compliance with the Law and other applicable legislation, and limited to the purposes listed above, to:

- legally authorized public institutions and organizations,
- courts, enforcement offices and other competent authorities,
- group companies, business partners, suppliers and service providers from whom support is received in recruitment, information technologies, security, legal consultancy or HR processes,
- authorized private institutions where required by legislation or by the nature of the process.

## 6. METHOD OF COLLECTION AND LEGAL BASIS OF YOUR PERSONAL DATA

Your personal data may be collected by automatic, partially automatic or non-automatic means, provided that they form part of a data recording system, through channels such as:

- application forms,
- CV/résumé submissions,
- e-mail correspondence,
- interviews,
- company physical premises,
- website, online career portals or similar digital channels,
- reference checks,
- documents submitted by the applicant.

Such data are processed based on the legal grounds set out in the Law, including explicit legal provision, necessity for contract-related processes, compliance with legal obligations, legitimate interest, establishment/exercise/protection of a right, and explicit consent where required.

## 7. RETENTION OF PERSONAL DATA

Your personal data shall be retained for the period required by the relevant legislation or, where no specific period is set, for the period necessary for the purposes for which they are processed. At the end of the retention period, the data will be deleted, destroyed or anonymized in accordance with the relevant legislation and the Company’s retention and destruction policy.

## 8. DELETION, DESTRUCTION AND ANONYMIZATION OF PERSONAL DATA

If the reasons requiring the processing of personal data cease to exist, your personal data will be deleted, destroyed or anonymized ex officio by the Company or upon your request, provided that the relevant legal conditions are met.

## 9. RIGHTS OF THE DATA SUBJECT AND EXERCISE OF SUCH RIGHTS

### 9.1. Rights of the Data Subject
Pursuant to Article 11 of the Law, you may apply to the Company and request information regarding whether your personal data are processed and, if so, exercise the following rights:

- learn whether your personal data are processed,
- request information if your personal data have been processed,
- learn the purpose of processing and whether the data are used in accordance with that purpose,
- know the third parties to whom your personal data are transferred domestically or abroad,
- request correction of incomplete or inaccurate personal data,
- request deletion, destruction or anonymization where the reasons for processing no longer exist,
- request notification of correction / deletion / destruction operations to third parties to whom the data have been transferred,
- object to the emergence of a result against you through analysis of processed data exclusively by automated systems,
- request compensation for damage in case you suffer damage due to unlawful processing of personal data.
