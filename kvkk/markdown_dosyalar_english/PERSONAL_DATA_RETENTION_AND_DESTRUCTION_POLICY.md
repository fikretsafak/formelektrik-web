## 1. INTRODUCTION

According to Article 20 of the Constitution of the Republic of Türkiye, everyone has the right to request the protection of personal data concerning them. This Policy has been prepared by **Form Elektrik İnşaat Mühendislik Anonim Şirketi** (the **“Company”**) pursuant to Article 7 of **Personal Data Protection Law No. 6698** (the **“PDPL”**) and the **Regulation on the Deletion, Destruction or Anonymization of Personal Data**.

The purpose of this Policy is to explain:

- how retention periods are determined in compliance with the PDPL and the Regulation,
- under what periods and conditions personal data obtained from customers, visitors, employees, suppliers, subcontractors, shareholders, website users, prospective product and service buyers and other persons are stored,
- the basis of the processes for determining the maximum period required for the processing purpose and for deleting, destroying and anonymizing personal data,
- how personal data required to be kept are stored in accordance with the legislation,
- the recording environments in which personal data are stored and how fast, easy and effective access to stored data is ensured.

## 2. DEFINITIONS

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Personal Data | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable real person |
| Data Controller | The real or legal person determining the purposes and means of processing personal data and responsible for establishing and managing the data recording system |
| Data Subject / Relevant Person | The real person whose personal data are processed |
| Data Processor | Real or legal persons who process personal data on behalf of the data controller based on authorization and instructions, excluding units/persons solely responsible for technical storage, protection and backup |
| Explicit Consent | Consent given freely, regarding a specific subject and based on being informed |
| Recipient Group | The category of real or legal persons to whom personal data are transferred by the data controller |
| Personal Data Protection Board / Authority | The Board / Authority established under the PDPL |
| Law | Personal Data Protection Law No. 6698 |
| VERBIS | Data Controllers Registry Information System |
| Data Recording System | A recording system in which personal data are processed according to specific criteria |
| Processing of Personal Data | Any operation performed on personal data such as obtaining, recording, storing, preserving, altering, rearranging, disclosing, transferring, taking over, making available, classifying or preventing use |
| Personal Data Processing Inventory | Inventory describing the Company’s personal data processing activities, purpose, category, recipient group, data subject group, maximum retention period, potential transfer abroad and security measures |
| Destruction | Deletion, destruction or anonymization of personal data |
| Retention and Destruction Policy | This policy serving as the basis for determining maximum retention periods and performing deletion, destruction and anonymization |
| Periodic Destruction | Recurrent deletion, destruction or anonymization to be performed ex officio at repeated intervals set out in the policy when all processing conditions cease to exist |
| Recording Environment | Any environment in which personal data processed automatically or non-automatically as part of a data recording system are located |
| Redaction / Blackout | Processes such as crossing out, painting over or blurring personal data so that they can no longer be linked to an identified or identifiable person |
| Masking | Processes such as deleting, crossing out, painting over or replacing certain fields with stars so that they can no longer be linked to an identified or identifiable person |
| Anonymization | Rendering personal data impossible to associate with an identified or identifiable real person, even by matching with other data |

Additional data subject groups referred to in this Policy include customers / potential service recipients, visitors, employees, job applicants, suppliers, subcontractors, shareholders and third parties.

## 3. TITLES, UNITS AND DUTIES OF PERSONS INVOLVED IN RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION PROCESSES

All units and employees of the Company actively support the responsible units in implementing technical and administrative measures, training personnel, raising awareness, monitoring compliance and preventing unlawful processing or access.

In principle, the following roles are involved in retention and destruction processes:

| Unit / Role | Main Duty |
|---|---|
| Committee Chair | Management and communication responsibility |
| Information Technologies / Data Security Responsible | Responsibility for IT and data security measures |
| PDPL Risk Management, Policy and Procedure Responsible | Responsibility for policy, procedure and risk management compliance |

| PDPL Compliance and Audit Responsible | Monitoring compliance and conducting audits |

## 4. RECORDING ENVIRONMENTS

The Company stores personal data in the recording environments listed below.

### 4.1. Electronic Environments
- Servers
- Software and applications
- Information security devices and systems
- Databases
- Storage areas, backups and cloud / digital environments where permitted
- E-mail systems
- Cameras and digital access systems
- Portable devices and removable media where authorized

### 4.2. Non-Electronic Environments
- Paper files and archives
- Forms and contracts
- Personnel files
- Visitor records
- Hard-copy legal / commercial documents
- Locked cabinets and archive rooms

## 5. RETENTION OF PERSONAL DATA

The Company retains personal data for the period stipulated by legislation or, where no period is specified, for the period necessary for the processing purpose. During retention, all necessary technical and administrative measures are taken to ensure lawful and secure storage.

## 6. LEGAL REASONS AND PROCESSING PURPOSES REQUIRING THE RETENTION OF PERSONAL DATA

### 6.1. Legal Reasons Requiring Retention
Personal data may need to be retained due to obligations arising from, among others:

- the PDPL,
- Turkish Commercial Code,
- Tax Procedure Law,
- Labor Law and social security legislation,
- Occupational Health and Safety legislation,
- Code of Obligations,
- Consumer legislation,
- relevant secondary legislation,
- contractual obligations,
- statutes of limitation, legal claims and evidentiary requirements.

### 6.2. Processing Purposes Requiring Retention

#### 6.2.1. Planning and Execution of Human Resources Procedures, Policies and Processes
Examples include recruitment, personnel affairs, payroll, leave, benefits, occupational health and safety, training, performance and internal organizational processes.

#### 6.2.2. Planning and Execution of the Security of the Company and / or Third Parties in Business Relations with the Company
Examples include physical security, visitor processes, transaction security, information security, emergency management and risk management.

#### 6.2.3. Planning and Execution of Product and / or Service Processes Provided by or on Behalf of the Company
Examples include order, sale, delivery, technical support, supplier management, procurement and after-sales support processes.

#### 6.2.4. Planning and Execution of Commercial and / or Operational Activities Conducted by the Company
Examples include finance, accounting, reporting, legal follow-up, logistics, documentation, communication, request and complaint handling.

#### 6.2.5. Planning, Auditing and Execution of Commercial Life and Business Development Strategies
Examples include strategic planning, business development, audit, internal control and corporate management processes.

## 7. MEASURES FOR THE SECURE RETENTION OF PERSONAL DATA AND FOR PREVENTING UNLAWFUL PROCESSING OR ACCESS

### 7.1. Administrative Measures
The Company implements policies and procedures, role and authority matrices, confidentiality undertakings, disciplinary provisions, employee training and awareness activities, supplier / subcontractor controls, business continuity planning, breach management and internal audit mechanisms.

### 7.2. Technical Measures
The Company implements access controls, authorization controls, password and authentication systems, encryption where appropriate, network and application security, anti-virus and firewall measures, logging, backup, vulnerability and penetration tests, secure archiving and data masking / redaction where appropriate.

### 7.3. Supervision of Measures Taken for the Protection of Personal Data
The effectiveness of the measures taken is periodically supervised and updated where necessary.

## 8. DESTRUCTION OF PERSONAL DATA

Where all conditions requiring the processing of personal data cease to exist, personal data are deleted, destroyed or anonymized ex officio or upon the request of the data subject, in accordance with the law.

## 9. MEASURES TAKEN IN RELATION TO THE DESTRUCTION OF PERSONAL DATA

### 9.1. Methods for Deleting Personal Data
Deletion methods may include deletion from relevant software, deletion of user access, removal from searchable environments, deletion of records and deletion / anonymization of data in structured systems.

### 9.2. Methods for Destroying Personal Data
Destruction methods may include physically destroying printed materials, secure deletion from digital media, destruction of storage media, and irreversible removal from relevant systems.

### 9.3. Methods for Anonymizing Personal Data
Anonymization methods may include masking, aggregation, data derivation, deletion of identifying fields, redaction, blurring and similar methods that prevent re-identification.

## 10. RETENTION AND PERIODIC DESTRUCTION PERIODS

Retention periods are determined according to the relevant legislation, business process needs and the Company’s Personal Data Processing Inventory. At the end of the applicable retention period, the relevant data are subject to periodic destruction within the periods set out in the Policy.

## 11. RIGHT OF APPLICATION OF THE DATA SUBJECT

Data subjects may submit requests within the scope of Article 11 of the PDPL using the Data Subject Application Form and the application methods announced by the Company. Requests are evaluated and concluded in accordance with the PDPL.

## 12. PERIODIC DESTRUCTION PERIOD

The Company performs periodic destruction at regular intervals defined in this Policy whenever all processing conditions cease to exist for the relevant personal data.

## 13. PUBLICATION AND STORAGE OF THE POLICY

This Policy is published in physical and/or electronic environments and is kept accessible to relevant persons.

## 14. POLICY UPDATE PERIOD

The Policy is reviewed and updated when required by legislative changes, operational needs or compliance requirements.

## 15. EFFECTIVENESS AND REPEAL OF THE POLICY

This Policy enters into force on the date of publication and remains effective until repealed or replaced by an updated version.
